-Reflection-

During this semester there have been many ups and many downs, and many stalls brought on through my own procrastination. I like to look back and imagine this semester in this class as a bit of a Role-Playing Game (RPG). I entered the class as a Level 0, and I am leaving the class as a Level 2, with the max level being put at 5. I had many experiences with the students in the room that I really wouldn't trade for the world, much like in an MMORPG (Massive-Multiplayer-Online-Role-Playing-Game), I have picked up some vital information that I will carry with me throughout the rest of my journey as a student of Kent State University, much like finding some awesome loot after slaying the big-evil monster during a certain stage. Most of all I feel as if I am leaving this class with an experience that cannot be changed, or given to anyone else, because it was so close to me and me only. I made the choice to come to Kent State, and I made the choice to enroll in the College Writing I class with the professor being Alexis M Baker. Much like an RPG where you will choose a certain character, embark on massive quests that take you down a long and spiraling path and you finally even up right where you should be, with every experience behind you, such a great memory to have, and yet not many share the same memory. I know this may not make sense in context, but it makes sense to me.
